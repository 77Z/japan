My Trip To Japan

By: Lauren Edwards

DAY ONE --------------------------

When I was coming on the plane I think a main question is what are great tourist attractions in Japan? We arive at the Kiyomizu-dera which holds a very stange tradition, in Japan Kannon the god of mercy is a beleif people go to this Shrine, before the law was changed in 1864 people would do this thing called "Taking the Plunge" which is a very advanced form of wish making pretty much you jump from the 13 meter building, and don't die. If your put life of the hands of Kannon and jump, you will be granted a wish! Unless you don't die (Because Kannon doesn't give wishes to corpses) According to the manuscript, 234 people jumped from the stage of Kiyomizu. A woman tried to jump even twice. Survival rate was about 85% (34 people died). After visting the shrine we go to the Shiono (塩野) in Akasaka which serves Mochi.Mochi is Japanese rice cake made of mochigome, a short-grain japonica glutinous rice, and sometimes other ingredients such as water, sugar, and cornstarch. The rice is pounded into paste and molded into the desired shape. Then we head down the streets of Dōtonbori a poular street know for it's food, shops and theater! Stop at a restaurant and crash into bed.

DAY TWO ------------------------

Waking up knowing today is a day to see all the beauty in Japan! The first part of today is to grab a quick breakfest at Kanetanaka So 金田中 草 [Shibuya] then heading to Chūbu region, Honshu, Japan the national park that has Mount. Fuji, a truly beautiful sight it's last Last eruption was on December 16, 1707 for now it's been dormant. Heading over to the studio ghibli museum,(jib-ly) pretty much if you love any form of art of Ghibli or Ghibli movie, Spirited Away, My Neighbor Totoro, Princess Mononoke, Howl's Moving Castle, Kiki's Delivery Service you will love this place. Then in the museum there is a cafe called The Staw Hat Cafe which serves really good food that is also seasonal so the menu depends on what season you come in. Over to the Narusawa Hyoketsu which is an ice cave, 70 foot lava cave with blue glowing ice crystal. While you walk you will see crystal up to 3 feet long! After we go to the very famous restaurant from the Japenese anime called "Naruto" the ramen restaurant is called Ichiraku Ramen Shime Shiten, their most famous ramen is called Miso Chashu Pork bowl. Then go to the hotel to sleep and think of the next day.

DAY THREE --------------------------

Waking up changing into a nice skirt, we head out to Shunsai 旬彩 breakfest place, Located on the 25th floor of the Shibuya Excel Hotel Tokyo, it has a stunning view of the city. The breakfast sets are around ¥3,000 and come with the season's fish and vegetables. Then we take a trip to the Fuji Five Lakes to hike up part of Mount Fuji and enjoy the view, and take in the freash air. After that I head over to a peace Memorial called Hiroshima in honor of the army men who died. And take a taxi to the Sushi Masuda an amazing place with great sushi! Then I meet up with my friend at the Kawaii Monster Cafe to eat a burger and we head over to Tokyo DisneyLand, which has a lot of cool colorful rides and everyone is so nice there I loved all the rides. Then in the park before I left I ate Little Green Alien Mochi Dumplings with Mike Wazowski Melon Bread and Afternoon Tea. Later we head over to Rainforest Cafe Tokyo and watch the volcano erupt and eat ジャイアントチョコボルケーノ (Chocolate Volcano ) And for the last thing to do I'm a nerd so I LOVE this place The Pokémon Center! It's full of Pokémon plush, Pokémon shirts, Pokémon everything! (I totally didn't spend ¥ 8000) And head back to the hotel.

DAY FOUR ------------------------------

Today was the last day before I head back to the USA and sleep, I want to make it worth it! Skip breakfest and head over to Tōdai-ji a old Buddhist temple complex that was once one of the powerful Seven Great Temples, located in Nara, Japan. It was so cool and I learned a lot of history from it, then we head down Takeshita Street with shops, cafes, and vending machines and stop at a few stores and take a taxi over to Robot Restaurant it was cool and VERY colorful with all it's lights, the food was fantastic and the peach juice was in this flashing rainbow lightblub and it was cool. Then I went over to the Fushimi Inari Taisha which is a Shinto shrine, Inari was originally and remains primarily the kami of rice and agriculture very pretty turns out to have as many as 32,000 sub-shrines (分社 bunsha) throughout Japan. Next, arcade! Sega GiGO is one of the most popular arcades in Tokyo, Japan it has claws, figher games, first person, and so much more! On the last part of our jounrey we head to Narita International Airport were we set of on our journey to go home.

Thank you so much for coming with me on this adventure, have a wonderful rest of your day!
